About
Hi there! 👋
I am Ivan Moriá Borges, a Brazilian Music Therapist with a Master's in Neurosciences, and a PhD Candidate currently serving as a Professor at UFMG (2025–2027).
As a multi-instrumentalist and researcher, I work at the intersection of Music Therapy, Neurosciences, and Computational Music Analysis. My work focuses on Music Information Retrieval (MIR) and the development of digital tools for micro-analysis and clinical assessment in music therapy. By leveraging automated data extraction and MIDI analysis, I explore how technology can enhance our understanding of social cognition and optimize therapeutic processes.
🔬 Research & Academic Background
🎓 Substitute Professor in Music Therapy (UFMG, 2025–2027): Teaching Ear Training, Harmony, and Improvisation for undergraduate music therapy students.
🎓 PhD Candidate in Music (UFMG, 2021-2026): Developing digital tools in Python, MAX/MSP, and JavaScript for MIDI data processing and statistical analysis, focused on Digital Music Therapy Assessment and open-access clinical microanalysis.
